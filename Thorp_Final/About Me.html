About Me



Nice to Meet You!

My name is Dominic Thorp, and I am currently a Sophomore at the University of Central Florida. For a good portion of my life, I would say up until 7th grade, I never really knew what I wanted to be as an adult. Growing up, I had friends that I enjoyed playing basketball with and who wanted to make it to the NBA as professional basketball players. I was around them so much that it caused me to mistake their dream for my own. I indulged in it for a few years until I eventually realized that it was not my passion. I did, however, spend a lot of time playing video games, watching anime, and drawing. These three things made me realize how much I value creating art and exploring my creativity as a whole. Despite my love for these things, I still need plenty of practice to develop my skills, and to achieve my goal of becoming a videogame designer, I decided to major in Computer Science. However, this was because I was informed that Computer Science could serve as a gateway to becoming a game developer or designer, and eventually, I realized that the major involves a lot of advanced math skills and coding. I am not the best at math, but the problem lies with the fact that it would not satisfy my desire to work with the creative aspects of game design. I then found out about the Emerging Media major, and eventually Digital Media within a few months as it fits my goals better as a major. Although I still have fears that I may not become as skilled as others in time, I am going to focus on myself and what I can do to the best of my ability in order to succeed. This major requires that I take drawing classes and other design courses, things that I know can help me in the meantime. I can also use any writing skills and writing research throughout my academic career to discover various techniques that will aid me in storytelling for whatever game I decide to create (or help create). As I continue to improve my writing capabilities and artistic prowess, I will ensure that I become proficient enough to become a game designer in the future.
